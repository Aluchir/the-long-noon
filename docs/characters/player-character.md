# The Newcomer (player character)

## 1. Snapshot
**Essence:** the first genuinely new consciousness since the Long Noon — and, though no one knows it, the last mortal being in the world.
**Role:** protagonist, player-controlled, player-named.
**First met:** the opening of the game, waking in **The Sunhollow**.

## 2. Surface
You wake in a sunny meadow at the edge of a gentle village, with no memory of arriving and no one expecting you. The villagers are warm but incurious — nobody asks where you came from, because nobody asks anyone anything. You're handed a tool, shown a workbench, pointed at the overgrown Bloom, and folded into the cozy rhythm of the place as if you'd always been there. On the surface, you are simply the newest resident of a lovely town with chores to do.

## 3. The dagger
You are **new** — the first person to *begin* in an age where no one is born and no one ends. That is why you feel things the others have lost: curiosity, restlessness, the pull to ask *why*, and eventually grief. They were sanded smooth by centuries; you arrived with them intact, sharp.

And the deepest cut, revealed only in Act 3: **you are mortal.** You age, almost imperceptibly. You can be hurt in ways that don't heal back to baseline. One day, alone among everyone who has ever lived, *you will die.* The cozy game has been, the whole time, the story of the world's last mortal walking gently through a paradise that forgot what you are.

> Why this works: it reframes every cozy hour in retrospect. The warmth wasn't naïve — it was the last mortal being briefly, beautifully alive.

## 4. Appearance & visual design
- **Customizable** (body, features, palette) so players own them — but with one fixed, near-subliminal **tell**: the Newcomer's **shadow is slightly longer and warmer-edged** than everyone else's, as if lit by an older sun. Players rarely notice consciously; it reads as "off" before the reveal explains it.
- Stylized, rounded, cozy-game proportions consistent with the cast.
- **Late-game subtle shift:** as mortality surfaces, tiny authored details creep in — a first faint line at the eyes, a slightly slower idle — never grotesque, just *real* in a world that isn't.
- Outfit/gear visibly upgrades with crafting tier (also a progression read for the player).

## 5. Personality & voice
- **Silent protagonist** with expressive, wordless **barks** (effort, surprise, delight, unease) and emotive animation — keeps the player projecting onto them, standard for cozy games.
- Characterized through *choices and reactions* (dialogue options, what you choose to investigate) rather than scripted lines.
- The framing voice in journal/codex entries is quietly **curious and tender** — the Newcomer notices what others walk past.

**Sample journal lines (codex voice):**
- *"Everyone here is kind. No one here is curious. I can't tell yet if that's peace or something missing."*
- *"The Keeper cried at the monument today and couldn't tell me why. I keep thinking about it. They don't."*
- *"My shadow falls wrong. I've started watching for others like it. There aren't any."*

## 6. Backstory & lore
The Newcomer's origin is the game's quiet central mystery. Working canon: the Long Noon did not *only* stop death — it stopped *beginning,* too. After untold ages, the world produced one new thing: you. Whether that's the world healing, the Gardener's experiment, an error in a perfect system, or the first sign the stasis is failing is the thread the player pulls. The Rememberer holds the answer; the Gardener confirms it.

## 7. Gameplay role & mechanics
- Drives the full loop: **gather → craft → build → reclaim the Bloom → explore → unlock regions.**
- Carries and upgrades **reclamation tools** (non-lethal; see Sprint-1 decisions §3).
- Has the **Tend** upkeep meters (Stamina/Focus, Comfort, tool durability) — never a death state (§4 of decisions).
- Holds the hidden **mortality** thread surfaced late — thematic, not punishing.
- Is the only character who can **read recovered lore fragments with comprehension** and gradually learn the forgotten script — mechanically, the player is the world's memory.

## 8. Arc across the game
1. **Sunhollow (R1):** the cozy newcomer. Learns the loop, feels the first faint wrongness.
2. **Overgrowth → Stillworks (R2–R3):** curiosity hardens into investigation. Realizes the world has forgotten itself.
3. **The Hush (R4):** confronts how deep the forgetting goes; possibly finds the Rememberer.
4. **The First Garden (R5):** meets the Gardener, learns what they are and what was taken, and makes the **final choice** (decisions §2).

## 9. Relationships
- **The Tinker:** first friend and mentor; the Newcomer's curiosity gently unsettles them.
- **The Keeper:** the Newcomer is the first being in an age to *ask why* the Keeper grieves — the start of the whole unraveling.
- **The Rememberer:** mirror and key — the only other person who carries truth; the Newcomer carries *feeling*, the Rememberer carries *memory*.
- **The Gardener:** sees the Newcomer as both its proudest result and the one thing it can't explain.

## 10. Easter eggs & deep-lore hooks
- The **shadow tell** is consistent from minute one — dataminers and re-players will find it was always there.
- A hidden **mirror/still-water** interaction late game lets the player notice the faint aging first, before the story says it aloud.
- The Newcomer's footprints in the Bloom **regrow slower** than NPCs' — the world treats them subtly differently, a buried hint they don't belong to the stasis.
