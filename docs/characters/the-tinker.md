# The Tinker

## 1. Snapshot
**Essence:** the village's cheerful builder and your crafting mentor — who keeps building because building is the only thing that still feels like time passing.
**Role:** crafting/building tutorial guide; recurring friendly NPC; emotional on-ramp to the cast.
**First met:** early in **The Sunhollow**, at their workshop.

## 2. Surface
The Tinker is delight in a person. They run the village workshop, greet you like an old friend they've somehow only just met, and can't wait to show you how to make things. They teach you your first tool, your first craft, your first building piece, narrating it all with infectious good cheer. Warm, generous, a little scatterbrained, endlessly enthusiastic about the next project.

## 3. The dagger
The Tinker has rebuilt the same workshop, by their own happy admission, "more times than I can count" — and finds it brand new every single time. In an immortal world with eroding memory, **building is the one thing that gives them the sensation of moving forward through time.** Finish a thing, forget you made it, make it again, feel progress. They aren't a hobbyist; they're a person using craft as a quiet, unknowing defense against the formless eternity of not-dying. If they ever stopped building, they'd have to feel how long they've actually been here. They never stop.

## 4. Appearance & visual design
- Cozy, rounded, perpetually mid-project: tool-belt, smudges, sleeves shoved up, hair held back with whatever was nearest.
- Workshop is immaculately organized in a way that suggests it's been organized *thousands of times* — every tool has a worn outline where it always goes.
- Visual easter egg: faint, layered "ghost" foundations around the workshop where previous identical builds once stood.

## 5. Personality & voice
- Bright, fast-talking, kind, easily distracted by a new idea mid-sentence.
- Never melancholy on the surface — the melancholy is structural, not spoken.

**Sample lines:**
- *"New hands! Oh, I love new hands. Here, hold this, no — this. Right! Now we make something."*
- *"I've built this bench before, I think? Doesn't matter. Building it now is the good part."*
- (late, if pressed) *"What did I do before all this building? ...Funny. I don't keep that. I keep the next thing."*

## 6. Backstory & lore
Pre-Noon, fragments suggest the Tinker may have been something like an engineer or maker — one of the few whose *habit* survived the memory-erosion because it lived in the hands, not the mind. Muscle memory outlasts memory. They are, in a sense, the healthiest-coping person in the village, which is its own quiet tragedy.

## 7. Gameplay role & mechanics
- **Tutorializes** crafting and building; unlocks the first workbench and tool tiers.
- Acts as a recurring **upgrade vendor/teacher** — new gear tiers are "unlocked" through them narratively.
- Gives early **fetch/build requests** that double as soft tutorials for gathering and the Bloom.

## 8. Arc across the game
- R1: pure mentor and friend.
- Mid-game: the player can choose to ask about their past; the Tinker's cheerful deflection becomes quietly heartbreaking once you understand it.
- Endgame: depending on the final choice, the Tinker is among those most changed — restoring death/memory means they'd finally remember everything they built, and lost.

## 9. Relationships
- **The Newcomer:** their favorite person; new hands mean a new audience for the joy of making.
- **The Forager:** trade partner and gentle friend; they keep each other busy.
- **The Keeper:** the Tinker quietly avoids the monument and doesn't know why it makes them uneasy.

## 10. Easter eggs & deep-lore hooks
- Counting the ghost-foundations around the workshop hints at how *many* times they've rebuilt — and thus, obliquely, how long the Long Noon has lasted.
- A locked drawer in the workshop, openable late, holds a single tool they never use and can't bring themselves to scrap — the only object that predates the Noon. They don't know why they keep it.
