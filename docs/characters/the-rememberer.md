# The Rememberer

## 1. Snapshot
**Essence:** the one person whose memory never eroded — who remembers every life and every death, and that no one else does, and cannot bear it.
**Role:** secret/optional character; the key to the True Ending; the player's mirror.
**First met:** hidden in **The Hush** (R4), only if the player has pursued the deep-lore threads.

## 2. Surface
If you find them at all, the Rememberer is withdrawn, careful, and startled that you came looking — startled, even, that you *could.* Where everyone else is pleasantly empty, the Rememberer is *full,* and it shows: they're guarded, weary, and watch you with an intensity no one else in the world has. They don't volunteer much. They're testing whether you're real — whether you're the new thing the world finally made.

## 3. The dagger
The Long Noon stopped death and frayed everyone's memory to a decade or so — **except theirs.** For reasons even they don't fully know (a fluke, a resistance, a cruelty), the Rememberer kept *everything.* Every person who ever lived. Every name the Forager reinvents. Every death the One Who Counts tallies. The meaning of the Keeper's monument. The world before the Long Noon, in full, with no one left to share it. They hid because **remembering everything, forever, while everyone you remember has forgotten you and themselves, is a specific and unending hell.** They are the world's grief made conscious — the inverse of the Newcomer. You carry *feeling* without memory; they carry *memory* without anyone to feel it with.

## 4. Appearance & visual design
- Older-seeming in bearing though not in body (no one ages) — the *weight* reads, even if the face can't.
- Lives somewhere deliberately hard to reach in the Hush, surrounded by the only intact records in the world — real texts, in the full forgotten script, that they can actually read.
- Visual mirror to the Newcomer: where the player's *shadow* falls wrong, the Rememberer's *eyes* do — too much behind them.

## 5. Personality & voice
- Precise, melancholic, dryly kind once they trust you; speaks like someone who has had every conversation before and is moved that this one is new.
- The only character who can give the player *straight answers* — and chooses, carefully, how much truth a person can hold.

**Sample lines:**
- *"You came looking. Do you understand how long it's been since anyone could? Since anyone *wanted* to know a thing badly enough to find me?"*
- *"I remember your Keeper's monument. I remember the names on it. I remember the people the names were. I am the only one who does. Do you see why I hid?"*
- *"You feel. I remember. Neither of us was supposed to exist. Between the two of us, we are almost a whole person who could survive the truth."*
- (toward the choice) *"Don't let it give death back blindly. And don't let it keep the lie. There's a third thing. Give them back their *memory.* Let each of them choose what you and I never got to."*

## 6. Backstory & lore
The Rememberer is the game's living library and its moral center. Finding them converts scattered fragments into coherent truth and, crucially, **unlocks the Third Way ending** (Sprint-1 decisions §2) — the option to return memory rather than death wholesale, letting the world choose for itself. They are also the proof that the Gardener's gift was flawed: one human the "kindness" couldn't fully reach.

## 7. Gameplay role & mechanics
- **Optional, gated** behind deep-lore progress (recovered fragments, learning the script, completing the Keeper / Forager / Counter threads) — rewarding thorough players.
- Functions as the **lore-synthesis NPC:** turns the player's collected fragments into full revelations; teaches the remainder of the forgotten script.
- **Unlocks the True Ending path** and reframes the final conversation with the Gardener.

## 8. Arc across the game
- Absent/rumored until R4; a felt absence (other characters have ghost-traces of "someone who knows" they can't place).
- Found: shifts from guarded to entrusting; hands the player the truth and the third option.
- Endgame: their fate is the emotional payoff of the ending choice — in the Third Way, they are finally not alone in remembering.

## 9. Relationships
- **The Newcomer:** mirror and counterpart; the only two beings who don't fit the stasis. Their scenes are the heart of the late game.
- **The Gardener:** the Rememberer is its incomplete result and its quiet indictment; they have, in a sense, been waiting ages for someone to carry the truth *to* it.
- **The Keeper & the One Who Counts:** the Rememberer knows exactly what those two are doing, and has never had the heart, or the right, to tell them. The player can.

## 10. Easter eggs & deep-lore hooks
- Hints of the Rememberer exist from R1 onward: a too-knowing line here, a record that shouldn't exist there — re-players find the breadcrumbs were always laid.
- The Rememberer's private records contain the **only complete pre-Noon history** in the game — an optional readable archive that rewards the most invested players with the full, unabridged world.
- A final, optional detail: the Rememberer remembers *the player waking up* — meaning they were watching for the Newcomer before the game even began.
