# Characters — roster & index

> Complete character bible for _The Long Noon_. One file per character.
> **Golden rule (from the World Bible):** every character is **cute first, dagger second.** The charming surface must land before the buried, devastating detail does. Never lead with the dagger.

## How to read these files
Each character doc follows the same structure so they're easy to hand to writers, artists, and designers:

1. **Snapshot** — one-line essence + role + where you meet them
2. **Surface** — how they read on first meeting (the cozy layer)
3. **The dagger** — the buried, devastating truth
4. **Appearance & visual design**
5. **Personality & voice** (with sample lines)
6. **Backstory & lore**
7. **Gameplay role & mechanics**
8. **Arc across the game**
9. **Relationships**
10. **Easter eggs & deep-lore hooks**

## Casting depth by region
- **The Sunhollow** (R1): the Tinker, the Keeper (early) — warm, welcoming, the cozy promise.
- **The Overgrowth** (R2): the Forager — first cracks in the pleasant surface.
- **The Stillworks** (R3): the One Who Counts, the Keeper (deeper) — the count, the monument's purpose.
- **The Hush** (R4): the Rememberer (if found) — coherence breaks down here.
- **The First Garden** (R5): the Gardener — the truth and the choice.

## Naming convention
Post-Long-Noon people have **no family names and no inherited names** — lineage is gone. Most go by a **role-title** the player learns them as (the Tinker, the Forager). Real personal names, where they exist at all, are fragments the player can uncover as lore. The player character (the Newcomer) is named by the player.
