# The Forager

## 1. Snapshot
**Essence:** the world's expert on the Bloom, who has lovingly named every plant in it — never knowing she's reinventing a language that already existed and was lost.
**Role:** gathering mentor; guide to the Bloom and its materials; the player's lens on the Overgrowth.
**First met:** **The Overgrowth** (R2), at the treeline where the Bloom thickens.

## 2. Surface
Calm, grounded, quietly joyful — the friend who knows every trail and every leaf. The Forager teaches you what's safe to harvest, what the Bloom is good for, and how to read the overgrowth. She has a private, charming name for everything: "ribbonleaf," "the shy ones," "morningspit," "grandmother-moss." She'll happily talk plants for hours.

## 3. The dagger
None of her names are real. The plants *had* names once — precise, shared, remembered names — and those are gone with everything else. So the Forager, needing to hold the living world in her hands, has been **quietly reinventing an entire lost language, alone, one plant at a time.** She thinks she's naming. She's grieving a vocabulary she doesn't know existed. And when the player later recovers fragments of the *original* plant-names in old texts, the gap between her loving inventions and the forgotten truth is one of the game's quietest gut-punches: she got so close, and never knew there was anything to get close to.

## 4. Appearance & visual design
- Earth-toned, practical, layered for weather; pockets full of cuttings; a worn satchel of pressed samples.
- Carries a hand-bound book of her own plant drawings and invented names — visibly a life's work, yet she'd tell you she "just started it a while back."
- Visual tell: her sample book has *far* more pages filled than any "while back" could explain.

## 5. Personality & voice
- Warm, unhurried, observant; speaks about plants the way people speak about old friends.
- Gently philosophical without realizing it.

**Sample lines:**
- *"This one I call morningspit — look how it beads at dawn. Had to call it something. Everything deserves a name."*
- *"Did it have a name before me? ...Huh. I suppose someone must have called it something, once. Doesn't come to me."*
- (late, on seeing a recovered true name) *"...That's a better name than mine. Older. Why does it feel like I'm remembering it?"*

## 6. Backstory & lore
The Forager represents the world's instinct to rebuild meaning from scratch — touching, doomed, and dignified. Her sample book is, functionally, a partial reconstruction of pre-Noon botany filtered through one person's love, and it becomes a genuine **lore artifact** the player can study.

## 7. Gameplay role & mechanics
- **Tutorializes gathering** and introduces the Bloom's material types and the prune/harvest distinction.
- Her sample book unlocks as a **field guide UI** — identifying plants, their uses, and (later) their recovered true names.
- Gives **gathering requests** that open up Overgrowth crafting materials and the next gear tier's components.

## 8. Arc across the game
- R2: friendly expert and guide.
- Mid: the player can cross-reference her names against recovered fragments — an optional lore mini-thread.
- Endgame: in a death/memory-restored ending, she'd finally learn the real names — and that everything she made was an act of mourning. Bittersweet.

## 9. Relationships
- **The Newcomer:** enjoys their genuine curiosity; the only person who asks *why* she names things.
- **The Tinker:** supplies materials; easy companionable friendship.
- **The Bloom itself:** her closest relationship, honestly, and she'd say so without irony.

## 10. Easter eggs & deep-lore hooks
- A **collectible meta-thread:** matching her invented names to recovered original names across the game unlocks a quiet reward and a tender final book entry.
- One plant she refuses to name ("that one doesn't want one") turns out, in old texts, to be the plant associated with the Long Noon itself — instinct she can't explain.
