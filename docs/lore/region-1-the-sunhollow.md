# Region 1 — The Sunhollow

> The starting region and the **vertical slice**. Everything the game is — cozy on top, devastating underneath — must be provable here in miniature. If the Sunhollow works, the game works.

## 1. Snapshot
- **Role:** tutorial region + emotional first impression + vertical slice.
- **Gate to enter:** none (start here).
- **Gate to leave (→ The Overgrowth):** craft the first reclamation tool tier (basic pruning blade) + complete the Tinker's opening build.
- **Surface mood:** idyllic. Warm meadows, a gentle village, soft golden light, friendly faces.
- **Buried layer:** the monument no one understands; the first faint sense that the warmth is covering something.

## 2. The place
A shallow sunlit valley — a "hollow" cupped in gentle hills, perpetually bathed in the kindest light in the whole world. A small village sits at its heart: cozy homes, the Tinker's workshop, the Forager's path to the treeline, and, at the center, **the monument** the Keeper tends.

The Bloom here is at its gentlest and most beautiful — wildflower meadows, soft mossy overgrowth, nothing threatening. It's the Bloom as a garden, not yet as a problem. This teaches the player to gather and prune in a forgiving sandbox.

**Landmarks:**
- **The village green & the Tinker's workshop** — crafting/building hub, first home plot.
- **The monument** — weathered, inscribed in the forgotten script, tended daily by the Keeper. The emotional seed of the entire game.
- **The Forager's treeline** — where the Bloom thickens and the path to Region 2 begins.
- **The player's plot** — a small buildable home space, the cozy anchor.

## 3. The mood, and the first crack
The Sunhollow is designed to be *genuinely lovely* for hours. The player should want to live here. Then, gently, the crack:
- The Keeper weeps at the monument and can't say why.
- No one can read the inscription. No one finds that strange.
- No one asks the player where they came from.
- The player's own shadow falls subtly wrong (the mortality tell — see [player character](../characters/player-character.md)).

Nothing is *explained* in the Sunhollow. The region's job is to plant the question, not answer it: *why is everyone so kind and so empty?*

## 4. Characters present
- **The Tinker** — crafting/building mentor; first friend. ([file](../characters/the-tinker.md))
- **The Keeper** — tends the monument; the first "something is wrong." ([file](../characters/the-keeper.md))
- **The Forager** — met at the treeline, bridges into Region 2. ([file](../characters/the-forager.md))

## 5. What it teaches (tutorial beats, diegetic)
1. **Movement & exploration** — wandering the cozy meadow.
2. **Gathering** — the Forager shows you how to harvest gentle Bloom.
3. **Crafting** — the Tinker teaches your first tool + workbench.
4. **Building** — claim and decorate your plot (the cozy hook + the Comfort meter).
5. **Reclamation (gentle)** — prune your first overgrown patch; meet a single, harmless animate Bloom-growth to learn the non-lethal subdue.
6. **The Tend meters** — Stamina/Focus and Comfort introduced softly.
7. **The codex** — your first lore fragment (an unreadable scrap) enters the journal, establishing the rediscovery loop.

## 6. Lore fragments seeded here
- The **monument inscription** (unreadable now; the long-game payoff).
- A scrap of the **forgotten script** the player files away (first language clue).
- The Keeper's unexplained grief (experiential, not text).
- An optional overheard line establishing "no one is born anymore" without the player understanding its weight yet.

## 7. The buried truth of the Sunhollow (for writers, not the player yet)
The monument is a **memorial to the pre-Noon dead** — the first, smallest instance of the world's forgotten mourning. The village's perfect contentment is the baseline state of the post-Noon world: pleasant, kind, hollow. The Sunhollow is the world *working as intended* by the Gardener — and that's exactly why it should feel faintly unbearable once the player understands.

## 8. Gameplay / production notes (vertical slice scope)
- **Smallest complete loop:** gather → craft tool → prune Bloom → build/decorate → unlock exit. Everything else in the game is an elaboration of this.
- **Bloom tier 1:** soft, forgiving, no real threat. One animate growth type for the subdue tutorial.
- **Reclamation tier 1:** the basic pruning blade.
- **Exit gate:** clear a thicket blocking the treeline path (requires the tool + a Tinker build) → opens The Overgrowth.
- **Art:** the warmest, brightest, most saturated golden hour in the game — the baseline the later regions erode away from.
- **Audio:** the warmest, fullest version of the main theme — the intact melody the later regions hollow out.

## 9. Connections
- **→ The Overgrowth (R2):** through the Forager's treeline, once tool + build gate is met.
- **Thematic callback:** the Sunhollow monument is recontextualized by the larger monument in The Stillworks (R3) and finally explained when the player can read the script.
