# Easter-Egg Catalogue

> Rewards for attention, re-players, and dataminers. The rule: every easter egg should *deepen the tragedy or confirm the lore,* never break tone with a joke that punctures the world. These are buried gifts for people who look hard — consistent with "the player rediscovers what the world forgot."

## Design principles
- **In-world, never winking.** No meme references, no fourth-wall gags. The world stays whole.
- **Reward attention, not luck.** Eggs reward noticing, counting, reading, and revisiting.
- **Retroactive payoff.** Many were "always there" — discoverable on replay, proving the game never cheated.
- **Tone-consistent.** Cute on the surface, dagger underneath — same as everything else.

## The catalogue

### Character / world details
- **The Newcomer's shadow** — falls slightly longer and warmer-edged than every NPC's, from minute one. The mortality tell, hidden in plain sight the entire game.
- **The Newcomer's footprints in the Bloom regrow slower** than NPCs' — the world subtly treats the one mortal thing differently.
- **The Tinker's ghost-foundations** — faint outlines of previous identical workshop rebuilds; counting them estimates how long the Long Noon has lasted.
- **The Tinker's locked drawer** — one tool, older than memory, never used, never scrapped; the only object predating the Noon they keep.
- **The Forager's unnamed plant** — the one she "won't name" turns out (in recovered texts) to be the plant tied to the Long Noon itself.
- **The Keeper's flowers** — exactly match a pre-Noon funerary plant named in the script; instinct outliving meaning.

### Counting / measuring eggs
- **The tally = pre-Noon population** — extrapolating the Hall of Marks matches a recovered census figure exactly. A reward for players who do the math.
- **The script-to-scratch gradient** on the tally wall — the marks degrade from language-glyphs to crude scratches across its length: a readable timeline of forgetting *how to count.*
- **Monument wear-grooves** — the Keeper's daily polishing has worn measurable grooves; their depth is another oblique clock of the Noon's age.

### Light & space eggs
- **The light points home** — in every region, the golden hour's angle subtly orients toward the First Garden, its true source. Obvious only once you know.
- **The lengthening light** — comparing screenshots region-to-region shows the sun's angle stretching and the palette desaturating by exact, consistent steps.
- **The Threshold's returning gold** — the only place before R5 where the light *brightens* again, pointing the way in.

### Script / language eggs
- **Pre-decodable script** — the glyph set is consistent enough that attentive players can decode words *before* the game formally teaches the tier.
- **The Gardener's single glyph** — *"the kindness I took away"* — appears, untranslated, in tiny places across all regions long before R5. Fluent re-players spot it everywhere, waiting.
- **The Rememberer remembers the player waking** — an optional late line reveals they were watching for the Newcomer before the game began.

### Audio eggs
- **The hollowing theme** — the main melody is the *same* across all regions, progressively losing notes (the "forgetting"); it returns whole only at the First Garden. Audiophiles will catch the through-line.
- **A Fading One's loop** — one near-erased NPC repeats half of a pre-Noon lullaby; the full version exists in the Rememberer's archive.

## Implementation notes
- Track discovery flags so a late-game **"Rediscovered"** codex section can quietly acknowledge the most-missed eggs (post-credits, spoiler-safe).
- Keep all eggs **datamine-friendly-consistent** — they should hold up under scrutiny and reward community lore-hunting (a marketing asset in itself).
- None of these gate progression; all are optional depth.
