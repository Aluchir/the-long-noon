# Region 5 — The First Garden

> The end. The source of the Long Noon, the resting place of the Gardener, and the stage for the final choice. Everything the player has gathered, built, learned, and felt converges here. This is not a boss arena — it is a conversation the whole game has been walking toward.

## 1. Snapshot
- **Role:** finale region; the truth, the Gardener, and the ending choice.
- **Gate to enter:** top-tier gear + key items (and, for the True Ending, the Rememberer questline from R4).
- **Gate to leave:** there is no "leave." The First Garden is the terminus; the player exits via the ending they choose.
- **Surface mood:** achingly, impossibly lovely. The golden light, fully restored and *radiant,* at its source. Perfect. Frozen. Unbearable.
- **Buried layer:** there is no buried layer left — this is where everything buried is finally on the surface.

## 2. The place
The epicenter of the Long Noon: a vast, breathtaking garden held in eternal golden radiance, where the light the player has watched lengthen and pale across four regions is finally **whole and brilliant** again. Time is most frozen here. Nothing moves that the Gardener does not move. It is the most beautiful place in the game and the most wrong — beauty with no breath in it, a perfect held note that never resolves.

**Landmarks:**
- **The approach** — a slow, reverent walk inward; light puzzles and lore convergence, not combat. The game decelerates on purpose.
- **The Gardener's resting place** — the heart, where the player finally meets the Gardener (or its fragment). ([character file](../characters/the-gardener.md))
- **The Seedbed** — where, depending on the ending, the player either lets ending/memory back into the world or seals the stasis.

## 3. The climax
The First Garden is built around a single conversation and a single choice:
- The player meets the **Gardener** — gentle, anxious, proud, alien. It asks: *is everyone happy now? Did I do it right?*
- The player understands at last: the catastrophe was an act of love by something that never understood what it took. There is no villain. That is the horror and the heartbreak.
- The Gardener offers to **undo it** — to give back ending, and with it grief, family, love, memory, meaning — *if the player can tell it that's what the world would want.*

### The three endings (see [Sprint 1 decisions](../SPRINT-1-DECISIONS.md) §2)
1. **The Gift Returned** — restore death and all it anchors. The world becomes mortal again: it will hurt, and it will mean something. Bittersweet, not "good" or "bad."
2. **The Long Noon Continues** — preserve the painless, hollow stasis. The Newcomer keeps the truth, and remembers, alone.
3. **The Third Way (True Ending)** — gated on the Rememberer: give the world back its **memory,** not death wholesale, and let each person *choose* for themselves whether to remember and become mortal again. The hardest to reach, the most hopeful.

## 4. Characters present
- **The Gardener** — the climax; the cause; the kindest catastrophe. ([file](../characters/the-gardener.md))
- **The Rememberer** (if the questline is complete) — present for the Third Way, finally not alone in remembering. ([file](../characters/the-rememberer.md))

## 5. What it is (mechanically)
1. **No boss fight.** A combat finale would betray the theme. The climax is presence, dialogue, light, and choice.
2. **Lore convergence** — every thread (monument, tally, machines, true-names, script) resolves; the player reads the Gardener's own words in the now-fully-learned script.
3. **The choice mechanic** — a deliberate, weighty, non-twitch interaction at the Seedbed.
4. **Ending sequences** — three distinct, authored epilogues showing the world after the choice.

## 6. Lore — the full truth (now fully surfaced)
- The Gardener arrived an age ago and removed death as mercy → the Long Noon, the end of birth, the erosion of memory, the runaway Bloom.
- It has waited, tending, ever since, to learn if it did right.
- The Newcomer is the one new, mortal variable it did not foresee — and the only one who can answer its question.
- The Rememberer is its incomplete result — the one mind its gift never fully reached.

## 7. The buried truth of the First Garden (writers only)
There is none left to bury — that's the design. Every region before this hid its truth under cuteness; the First Garden is the one place where the surface and the depth are the same thing. Its beauty is no longer a disguise; it's the honest face of a perfect, painless, meaningless eternity. The player's final act is to decide whether that's mercy or theft. The game refuses to tell them which. **The dissonance the whole game was built on resolves into a question, not an answer.**

## 8. Gameplay / production notes
- **No combat finale.** Decelerated, reverent traversal + light puzzles + the conversation.
- **The choice** must feel earned and heavy — pace it, give it silence, no UI clutter.
- **Three authored endings** with distinct epilogue scenes; the Third Way requires the Rememberer flag.
- **Art:** the golden hour fully restored and radiant — callback to the Sunhollow's warmth, now revealed as having always emanated from here. The most beautiful, most still frame in the game.
- **Audio:** the main theme returns **whole** for the first time since the Sunhollow — but transformed by everything the player now knows; each ending gets its own final variation (mortal/bittersweet, frozen/lonely, hopeful/shared).

## 9. Connections
- **← The Hush (R4):** through the Threshold.
- **Callbacks:** closes the Sunhollow (the light's true source), the monuments and tally (the dead the choice concerns), and the Newcomer/Rememberer mirror.
- **→ Endings:** the player exits the game through the choice they make here.
