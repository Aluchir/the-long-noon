# Region 2 — The Overgrowth

> Where the cozy promise of the Sunhollow gets its first real complication. Still beautiful, still mostly kind — but *too alive,* and the first place the world starts to feel wrong on purpose.

## 1. Snapshot
- **Role:** first true region beyond the tutorial; introduces the Bloom as a *problem*, not just scenery.
- **Gate to enter:** basic reclamation tool + opening build (from R1).
- **Gate to leave (→ The Stillworks):** mid-tier crafted gear + the ability to cut/still denser Bloom; a building requirement (a bridge/clearing the Tinker designs).
- **Surface mood:** lush, vivid, almost intoxicatingly green and golden — nature in joyful excess.
- **Buried layer:** the first dawning that this lushness is *unnatural* — that decay has stopped, and life here can't switch off.

## 2. The place
A dense, riotous forest-garden where the Bloom has run for an age without anything to check it. Towering overgrowth, hanging vines, flower-choked clearings, fungal groves that glow softly in the long amber light. It is genuinely gorgeous — and the deeper you go, the more it tips from "lovely" to "smothering." Paths close behind you as the Bloom regrows. Old structures are swallowed whole, visible only as suggestive shapes under the green.

The light here is a touch longer and more amber than the Sunhollow — the first step of the "lengthening light."

**Landmarks:**
- **The Forager's deep camp** — her base of operations and the hub for this region's gathering and field-guide work.
- **Swallowed structures** — pre-Noon buildings the Bloom has engulfed; the first ruins the player can partially excavate for lore.
- **The Choke** — a wall of ancient, animate Bloom blocking the way to The Stillworks; the region's exit challenge.
- **Glowing fungal groves** — atmospheric, a source of a key crafting material, and subtly *wrong* in how they never wilt.

## 3. The mood, and the deepening crack
The Overgrowth is where the player first feels the world's central wrongness in their hands rather than just their heart:
- Cut the Bloom and it visibly **regrows** faster than anything natural should.
- Nothing here is dead. No fallen logs rotting, no wilted flowers — everything is endlessly, relentlessly alive.
- The Forager's plant-names (lovely, invented) brush against the first **recovered true names** in the swallowed ruins, opening the quiet question: *who named these before, and where did they go?*

## 4. Characters present
- **The Forager** — region guide; gathering and the field-guide/true-names lore thread live here. ([file](../characters/the-forager.md))
- The Tinker may visit / send build requests remotely (outpost system).

## 5. What it teaches / introduces
1. **Bloom tier 2** — denser growth, the first animate growths that genuinely resist (still non-lethal).
2. **The "still" tool function** — temporarily returning a patch of Bloom to dormancy to pass or harvest it.
3. **Regrowth & the time-without-time loop** — areas reclose; the player learns to manage and re-clear.
4. **Outposts & fast travel (intro)** — establish the first outpost so backtracking to the Sunhollow isn't a slog.
5. **Ruin excavation** — clearing Bloom off swallowed structures yields materials *and* lore fragments.
6. **The true-names lore thread** — cross-referencing the Forager's invented names with recovered ones.

## 6. Lore fragments seeded here
- **Recovered true plant-names** (in the forgotten script) inside swallowed ruins — the first time the player sees the script attached to something *translatable* by context.
- Evidence that the Bloom is **post-Event** runaway growth, not the world's natural state.
- A fragment hinting that these swallowed buildings were *homes* — and that homes once held more than one kind of bond (an early, unexplained brush with the lost concept of family).

## 7. The buried truth of the Overgrowth (writers only)
The Overgrowth is the world's **second law made visible**: nothing decays, so life never stops. It's also the first graveyard of the *built* past — pre-Noon civilization, swallowed and forgotten. The Forager's loving renaming is the world trying, and failing, to rebuild lost meaning from scratch. The beauty is real; so is the suffocation. This region teaches the player to distrust loveliness.

## 8. Gameplay / production notes
- **Bloom tier 2:** denser, partially animate; introduces the still-mechanic and resist-and-subdue.
- **Regrowth systems** front-and-center: the player must manage reclaimed space.
- **First fast-travel outpost** established here.
- **Exit gate — The Choke:** a large animate Bloom mass requiring mid-tier gear + a Tinker-designed clearing/bridge build to get past → opens The Stillworks.
- **Art:** lusher and more saturated than anywhere — then the player descends and it never looks this alive again. The amber light is one notch longer than the Sunhollow.
- **Audio:** the main theme, fuller and more ornamented (over-grown, like the Bloom) — the *last* time it sounds abundant before the hollowing begins.

## 9. Connections
- **← The Sunhollow (R1):** via the Forager's treeline.
- **→ The Stillworks (R3):** through The Choke.
- **Thematic seed:** the swallowed *homes* here pay off in The Stillworks (purpose-built structures) and in the family/loss themes of the late game.
