# Region 3 — The Stillworks

> The midpoint, and the turn. The cozy game has lured the player this far; the Stillworks is where the buried story stops whispering and starts being *legible.* This is where the player first understands that the world is built on something it has forgotten.

## 1. Snapshot
- **Role:** the narrative pivot. Cozy systems are mastered; the story takes the wheel.
- **Gate to enter:** mid-tier gear (from R2) + the ability to clear The Choke.
- **Gate to leave (→ The Hush):** advanced gear, climbing/sealing tools, and a key item recovered here (a fragment of the forgotten script that "unlocks" deeper reading).
- **Surface mood:** abandoned-yet-tidy. Old infrastructure, quiet, oddly maintained. Less lush than the Overgrowth — the first place that feels *emptied* rather than overgrown.
- **Buried layer:** these are machines and halls **built to end things** — and the people who tend them no longer know what ending meant.

## 2. The place
A region of pre-Noon **infrastructure**: great quiet halls, dormant machinery, aqueducts and works of obvious purpose whose purpose is gone. The Bloom is thinner here, kept back, almost *managed* — by whom, and why, is unclear. Everything is unnervingly tidy, as if maintained out of habit by hands that don't know what they're maintaining.

The light has lengthened further — deep amber, long shadows, the gold beginning to feel less like morning-warmth and more like the last hour before a dusk that never comes.

**Landmarks:**
- **The Hall of Marks** — the vast chamber where the **One Who Counts** scores the dead into the walls. The signature set-piece of the region. ([character file](../characters/the-one-who-counts.md))
- **The Great Monument** — far larger than the Sunhollow's, recontextualizing it: these are memorials, and they are *everywhere.*
- **The Stillworks proper** — dormant machines whose recovered schematics reveal they were built to *process endings* (decay, burial, cycling) — functions a deathless world made meaningless.
- **The Keeper's deeper post** — the Keeper tends the Great Monument here, grief more visible, the mystery sharper.

## 3. The mood, and the reveal
This is where the player's accumulated unease resolves into comprehension:
- The **Hall of Marks** + an optional bit of environmental math reveals the tally equals the **pre-Noon population.** The dead were real, and numberless.
- The **machines** were built to handle *ending* — and now sit dormant and purposeless, because nothing ends.
- The **monuments** are revealed as a civilization-wide practice of mourning, performed by people who've forgotten what they mourn.
- The player can now read **more of the forgotten script**, turning fragments into sentences.

The Stillworks is the "oh — *oh*" region. The cute surface is now translucent; the player sees the grief underneath clearly for the first time.

## 4. Characters present
- **The One Who Counts** — the region's emotional core; the tally reveal. ([file](../characters/the-one-who-counts.md))
- **The Keeper** — deeper, more sorrowful here; the monument thread escalates. ([file](../characters/the-keeper.md))

## 5. What it introduces
1. **Bloom tier 3 + managed-Bloom puzzles** — the Bloom is held back here; the player learns *why* and how.
2. **Climbing / sealing tools** — new traversal for the vertical, built infrastructure (and the exit gate).
3. **Deeper script-reading** — a recovered key fragment lets the player read longer passages, retroactively unlocking meaning in earlier fragments (including the Sunhollow inscription).
4. **The dormant-machine lore puzzles** — recovering schematics that explain the world's lost relationship with ending.

## 6. Lore fragments seeded here
- **The tally = pre-Noon population** correspondence.
- **Machine schematics** describing functions tied to death/decay/cycling.
- **A readable passage** (first full sentences of the script) — likely a memorial dedication, connecting monument, tally, and the word for the thing the Gardener removed.
- First indirect references to **someone who remembers** (seeding the Rememberer in R4).

## 7. The buried truth of the Stillworks (writers only)
The Stillworks is the world's **machinery of mortality, switched off.** Before the Long Noon, this civilization had infrastructure for living *and ending* — and all of it was rendered purposeless in a single merciful act. The tidy maintenance is habit outliving meaning at industrial scale. The region's thesis: *the world was built for mortal beings, and mortal beings no longer exist — except, secretly, the player.* This is where the player should first feel the weight of what the Gardener did, before they ever meet it.

## 8. Gameplay / production notes
- **Bloom tier 3:** sparser but stranger; managed-Bloom puzzle mechanics.
- **New tools:** climbing + sealing, opening vertical infrastructure and the exit.
- **Set-piece:** the Hall of Marks — a quiet, awe-and-dread space; the optional counting puzzle is a major codex unlock.
- **Exit gate:** recover the script-key + use sealing/climbing tools to reach a sealed passage → opens The Hush.
- **Art:** the palette's first clear desaturation step; gold going bronze, shadows long. Tidy emptiness, not lushness.
- **Audio:** the main theme now noticeably **hollowed** — notes thinning, the warmth draining; the "forgetting" made audible.

## 9. Connections
- **← The Overgrowth (R2):** via The Choke.
- **→ The Hush (R4):** through a sealed infrastructure passage.
- **Payoffs:** recontextualizes the Sunhollow monument; sets up the Rememberer; the machines foreshadow the final choice (restoring "ending").
