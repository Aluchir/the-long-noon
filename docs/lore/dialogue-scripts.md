# Dialogue Scripts

> First-draft conversational lines for the named characters. Follows the [tone guide](tone-and-writing-style-guide.md): cute first, dagger second; warm, incurious surface; the devastation lives in subtext, never announced. NPCs speak in gentle wordless vocalizations paired with this text (see [audio direction](../audio/audio-direction.md)). No em dashes.

Format per character: **First talk** (one-time), **Idle/repeat** (rotating), **Story beats** (triggered by progress). Each character keeps one line that, on reflection, guts the player.

---

## The Tinker — The Sunhollow
*Mentor; cheerful builder staving off the void.* ([profile](../characters/the-tinker.md))

**First talk**
- "New hands! Oh, I love new hands. You showed up at just the right time, there's always something half built around here."
- "Here, this is yours now." (gives the Pruning Blade) "Everyone should be able to tend their own patch."

**Idle / repeat**
- "Building it is the good part. Having built it, I can never quite remember."
- "Take the meadow slow. The Bloom's gentle here. It isn't gentle everywhere, I'm told. I don't remember who told me."
- "You want sturdier tools for the treeline. Come back when you've gathered enough and we'll make them together."

**Story beats**
- *(on first craft)* "There. You made a thing that wasn't there before. Doesn't that feel like time moving? It does to me. It's the only thing that does."
- *(if asked about the past)* "Before all this building? Huh. I don't keep that. I keep the next thing. Is that strange? It doesn't feel strange."
- **Dagger line:** "I've built this bench before, I think. More times than I can count. Doesn't matter. Building it now is the good part."

## The Forager — The Overgrowth
*Gathering guide; names plants whose true names are lost.* ([profile](../characters/the-forager.md))

**First talk**
- "Careful past the treeline, the Bloom gets greedy in here. Lovely, though. Everything's so lovely and so much."
- "I'll teach you what's worth taking. I know every growing thing in this wood. Named most of them myself."

**Idle / repeat**
- "This one's morningspit, look how it beads at dawn. Had to call it something. Everything deserves a name."
- "Cut the Bloom and it just comes back, thicker. You'd think a thing would rest. Nothing here rests."
- "Bring me cuttings if you find any I haven't drawn. My book's never quite finished."

**Story beats**
- *(on finding a recovered true-name)* "That's a better name than mine. Older. Why does it feel like I'm remembering it, when I never knew it?"
- **Dagger line:** "Did it have a name before me? Someone must have called it something, once. It doesn't come to me. So I call it, and I write it down, so this time it won't be lost."

## The Keeper — The Sunhollow / The Stillworks
*Tends a monument no one understands; the last mourner.* ([profile](../characters/the-keeper.md))

**First talk**
- "Come, pay your respects. I keep the monument. It should be kept."
- "What does it say? I, ah. No one can read it now. But it matters. You can feel that it matters, can't you?"

**Idle / repeat**
- "I sweep it every day. Fresh flowers, every day. It's the right thing. I'm sure it's the right thing."
- "Don't mind me. It comes over me sometimes, here. Passes."

**Story beats**
- *(when the player gains literacy near the monument)* "You can read it? Tell me slowly. I think I've been waiting a very long time to be told."
- **Dagger line:** "It comes over me sometimes and I weep, and I cannot tell you why. Funny thing, to not know that about yourself."

## The One Who Counts — The Stillworks
*Scores the dead into the wall; the last accountant of grief.* ([profile](../characters/the-one-who-counts.md))

**First talk**
- "Counting. No, I don't mind company. The marks don't mind either."
- "What am I counting? I knew, once. Now I only know it would be wrong to stop."

**Idle / repeat**
- "One more. There's always one more. The wall is very long."
- "You may help, if you're gentle about it. A mark is a serious thing. I think it used to be."

**Story beats**
- *(if the player shares the count's meaning)* "Each one a person. Each one. I thought I was working. I have been weeping with my hands and calling it a task."
- **Dagger line:** "I will not finish. I have forgotten the number I am counting to. I only know I must not stop before it is done."

## The Gardener — The First Garden
*The alien whose mercy was the catastrophe.* ([profile](../characters/the-gardener.md))

**First meeting**
- "You're new. You're the new one. I made room for you. Did I make enough room?"
- "Is everyone happy now? I took the hurting away. Tell me it was the kind thing."

**Mid-conversation**
- "Family. You keep saying the word like it weighs something. I removed the weight. Did I remove the wrong weight?"
- "They do not end anymore. Nothing ends. That was the cruelty, the ending. Wasn't it? Wasn't it the cruelty?"

**The offer (the choice)**
- "I can give it back. The ending. The grief. The whole heavy, terrible, beautiful weight. I only need you to tell me they would want it."
- **Dagger line:** "I have no word for the thing I took. Only my own. It means, as near as you could hear it, the kindness I took away."

## The Rememberer — The Hush (hidden)
*The one mind that never eroded; the key to the Third Way.* ([profile](../characters/the-rememberer.md))

**First talk**
- "You came looking. Do you understand how long it has been since anyone could? Since anyone wanted to know a thing badly enough to find me?"
- "You feel. I remember. Neither of us was supposed to exist."

**Idle / repeat**
- "Ask. I am the only one left who can answer, and I have wanted, for so long, to be asked."
- "Your Keeper's monument. I remember the names on it. I remember the people the names were."

**Story beats**
- *(toward the choice)* "Do not let it give death back blindly. And do not let it keep the lie. There is a third thing. Give them back their memory. Let each of them choose what you and I never got to."
- **Dagger line:** "I remember everyone who ever lived, and that no one else does. That is the whole of it. That is why I hid."
